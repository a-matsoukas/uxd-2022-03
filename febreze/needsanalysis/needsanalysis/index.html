Needs Analysis Phase Submission

What problem are you proposing to solve?

The problem we are investigating is the difficulty in finding places that are accessible for people with mobility restrictions. This is especially challenging when in a new area and when things like elevators and automatic doors break. Our project will provide current, accurate accessibility information about locations with accessible facilities as well as where certain features may temporarily be broken. The image below is an interaction map of our design.

Who matters?

People with physical accessibility needs (i.e. mobility restrictions) are at the forefront of the experience we are designing for. In particular, we are targeting the subset of people who want to travel and explore new places, and not let their mobility restrictions get in the way of enjoying their travel experience. However, our project would also be useful for just about anyone — even if you don’t need things like elevators or ramps, they can be helpful for everybody.

How do your prospective users think about this problem/activity?

For our users, getting to a particular destination is oftentimes the hard part. However, once they get to their destination, it is difficult to know if nearby places that they want to visit have the accessible facilities they need to get around.

What requirements must your solution satisfy? (think about different stakeholders)

  • Fast — checking if a place is accessible should take a tiny amount of time, and reporting if something is broken should also be quick. If it takes too long to report, people won’t report it.
  • Accurate/up-to-date — going somewhere after our app says it’s accessible and then finding out that it isn’t, is a situation to avoid, unless something at that place broke recently.

What will the impact of a successful solution be?

If this solution is successful, our interface would be the go-to place for people with physical disabilities and mobility restrictions who want to travel to refer to in order to ensure accessible facilities are available.

What are the limitations of this design?

For this app to become a reality we would need a lot of data, both about the accessibility of specific locations and the ways of getting to them. For the purposes of this project, we are suspending disbelief and assuming we have enough users to provide this data. We are also assuming that we have both users with and without mobility restrictions so that our data doesn’t entirely rely on people with disabilities being inconvenienced. Furthermore, the disability community is extremely diverse even when only looking at mobility restrictions. While we want our app to inform a wide range of individuals, we know it will not encompass every possible accessibility need.
