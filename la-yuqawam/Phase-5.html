Critical Feedback

The most critical feedback we got from the heuristic evaluation were the lack of back buttons and exit options for the user, causing them to feel stuck, and the inconsistency in design (font, color, etc) across different interfaces. We addressed the lack of exit options issue by adding more back buttons specifically in the chat UI and the onboarding process to give users more freedom in these important sections. We also standardized our font and coloring across the different interfaces, sticking to Unna for prompts and titles and Inter for general fonts and a specific color palette for all of our interfaces.

Additional Feedback

Another feedback from the heuristic evaluation was that the similarity of our app with Tinder made the users feel uncomfortable when swiping on pets because of the fast-paced, meaningless connotations and concerns about objectifying the pets. Users were worried that potential adopters were scrolling through pets mindlessly based on appearance rather than if the pet and potential owner would be a good fit. We agreed that adoption is a very important decision that needs deep thought before a prospective owner commits, and based on this feedback, we decided to pivot our browsing flow from the tinder swipe to the Hinge tap to make it more intentional and decision driven.
