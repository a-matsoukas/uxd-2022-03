Problem Statement

Currently, the adoption process frustrates many potential pet owners and it results in people opting to acquire pets from unethical breeders, and people who adopt spontaneously and are looking to rehome their pets. Thus, many pets are harmed or abandoned during and/or after adoption, if they are even adopted at all.

Solution

Our solution is the ​Adögi app, which aims to make the adoption process easier and faster for shelters, pet rehomers, and potential pet owners. We provide a platform for both parties to meet with standardized information and help each other find the perfect pet/perfect home for a pet. 

Post adoption, our platform provides pet owners a place to hire animal-loving caretakers for pet-related services.
SUBMISSION

Goals

1

Enhance the way pet adoption is perceived and gone about by the general public.

2

Promote the "adopt, don't shop" mentality to discourage supporting unethical breeders.

3

Engage our users in building their profiles to obtain adoption information hassle-free.

4

Foster a seamless experience for animal lovers to pet sit for users.

Value Proposition

For people looking to adopt pets or rehome pets, our app is an accessible and efficient way to connect suitable adopters with their perfect pets according to their preferences.

Unlike animal shelters, our adoption process connects potential pet owners and pet re-homers/shelters with only those who match each party's requirements and preferences to expedite the search and approval process. Unlike current pet-finding apps, our app creates an extended ecosystem where pet-owners can find pet-lovers to care for their pets while they are busy or away. This allows pet-lovers a platform to find animal-related jobs, as well as making pet caretaking easier while encouraging pet owners to keep their pets when they get busy to discourage more re-homing.

We have three main stakeholders: current pet owners (individual/shelter/rescue), prospective pet owner, and the pet.

There is an overflow of shelter animals and strays in specific states, but people are still looking to breeders/pet stores to get their pets. We want to promote adoptions from shelters and rescues instead of specific breeds among pet owners and connect them to shelters, rescues, and foster families based on their pet preferences.

Oftentimes, being able to find a permanent home can be a matter of life or death for the pet: when shelters overflow, they would need to euthanize the animals. The current pet owner needs to balance 3 things: maximizing pet adoptions, minimizing pet returns, and continuing to function monetarily (mainly for rescues and shelters). Prospective pet owners tend to focus on reducing resources invested in searching for and owning a new pet, and finding the best personality fit and other preferences of the new pet.

A successful solution of ours would seek to standardize the adoption information needed by current pet owners while not making it tedious for the prospective owners. We also seek to enhance pet and prospective owner match to minimize returns (which benefits all 3 main stakeholders) while making the platform easy to use.

Lexicons

We defined the most common vocabulary used in the process of pet adoption to help communicate across the field Adögi encompasses. 

Adoption

A process in which a rescued pet is placed into a permanent home.

Animal Control

A (usually) local govt agency that responds to citizen calls regarding stray and/or dangerous animals and impounds them

Backyard Breeder

A person who chooses to breed their family pet for whatever reason suits them, not always profit.

Caregiver

A person overseeing and providing food and veterinary care for a feral cat colony.

Collector

A person who acquires and hoards many pets without providing proper care.

Commercial Breeder

Licensed breeders that breed multiple breeds for profit.

Cruelty

Physical harm or injury inflicted upon an animal by an individual or group of individuals.

Domestic

An animal, which has adapted to humans over many generations. Also called companion animals.

Ethical
Breeder

 A breeder that breeds to improve the breed, not for profit.

Euthanasia

Ending the life of a pet.

Feral Cat

A cat that was previously domestic but became lost or abandoned and reverted to a wild state.

Guardian

Politically correct term for a pet's owner.

Kill-shelter

A facility that euthanizes animals on a regular basis, usually animal control shelters.

Neglect

Depriving a pet (or pets) of the most basic necessities of food, water, shelter and veterinary care.

Neuter

The surgical removal of a male pet's testicles to prevent reproduction.

No-kill

A shelter or rescue's policy that any healthy or treatable animals will be given the opportunity and resources to live until adopted.

Pedigree

The family tree of a pet.

Puppymill

Commercial breeding establishments, usually unlicensed, that sell puppies to pet stores, at flea markets or through newspaper ads.
